    target_location: ".reviewcompass/specs/workflow-management/requirements.md Requirement 13 Acceptance Criteria 5"
    description: "AC5 introduces an unsupported addition by requiring approval for `repair_workflow_state` as a 'simple operation' with `approval_required: true`."
    rationale: "The upstream integrated design and mechanized execution notes treat workflow-state repair as a stop/repair path that must fail closed and be machine-detected, but they do not establish a human-approval gate for every repair action. Adding mandatory approval here changes responsibility boundaries and can block purely corrective state-repair operations that upstream material did not classify as human-approved irreversible actions."
  - severity: ERROR
    target_location: ".reviewcompass/specs/workflow-management/requirements.md Requirement 14 Acceptance Criteria 8-10"
    description: "The workflow-state snapshot requirements omit the upstream boundary that snapshot generation must be read-only and must not become a mutating state-management path."
    rationale: "The upstream decision materials distinguish state visualization from state mutation and require clear read-only versus mutating operation boundaries. Requirement 14 says the snapshot is a byproduct and not the source of truth, but it does not explicitly forbid snapshot generation/update from mutating workflow state or being used as a repair/write path. Without that prohibition, downstream design could drift into using snapshot save/update operations as hidden state mutation."
  - severity: WARN
    target_location: ".reviewcompass/specs/workflow-management/requirements.md Requirement 15 Acceptance Criteria 6-7"
    description: "Requirement 15 correctly positions the LLM judge as auxiliary analysis, but it does not explicitly state that judge output must not approve, clear, or override machine gates, human-only decisions, or operation-contract postconditions."
    rationale: "The upstream intent emphasizes that semantic audit is non-approving auxiliary analysis. The current text says final approval is not automated, but leaves room for downstream interpretation that a successful judge audit could substitute for other required checks or unblock progress. An explicit non-substitution boundary would better preserve the upstream forbidden-action constraint."

AC10 forbids mixing 'phase-spanning intermediate states' in a single commit, but it does not clearly forbid introducing dormant or partially wired later-phase behavior during earlier phases. That ambiguity weakens the phase-boundary intent and can make implementation review contentious."
